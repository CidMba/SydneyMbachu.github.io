OUR APPROACH

We believe that with the benefit of diverse perspectives, organizations will design better products, deliver better services, and build a better world.

We collaborate with leaders to develop a comprehensive diversity strategy aligned with the company's vision and values and designed to advance its broader goals. We then partner with stakeholders across the organization — from recruiting and retention to communications and policy — to bring that strategy to life.

Strategies
We partner with clients to develop a comprehensive diversity strategy. From developing and defining goals to designing and implementing new processes and policies, we draw on social science research to address the barriers that inhibit diversity.
Ongoing Advising
We partner with clients to implement and track strategies, monitoring impact and iterating to ensure effectiveness.
Workshops and Trainings
We design workshops that train employees, managers, and C-level executives to recognize and minimize biased behavior, contributing to more effective and inclusive organizational cultures. We emphasize action-oriented workshops that have a positive and measurable impact.

Joelle Emerson

Founder & CEO

As the Founder and CEO of Paradigm, Joelle partners with leaders of some of the world's most exciting and innovative companies to develop and execute effective diversity strategies. Joelle is an expert on diversity, inclusion, and unconscious bias, and has written on these subjects for USA Today, TechCrunch, and Cosmopolitan. Joelle's work has been featured in The New Yorker, Forbes, Marketplace, CNN Money, and several other outlets.

Before founding Paradigm Joelle was a women's rights employment lawyer. As a Skadden Fellow at Equal Rights Advocates, she represented women in gender discrimination and sexual harassment litigation, and she advocated for local, state, and federal policies to ensure equal pay and other workplace protections for women. Joelle's legal background highlighted for her the consequences that can result from companies failing to consider diversity and inclusion early, and inspired her to found Paradigm.

Joelle is an advisor to Women Who Code, and sits on Anita Borg Institute's Organizational Change Working Council. She is a graduate of Stanford Law School.

Natalie Johnson

Partner

As a Partner at Paradigm, Natalie uses her tech industry experience and organizational psychology background to design and execute diversity strategies. As a former People Analyst at Google, Natalie has experience developing and analyzing diversity initiatives, and was one of the original facilitators for Google's unconscious bias training. In this role, she led over 60 unconscious bias sessions in 5 countries, and trained over 30 facilitators globally.

In addition to her work at Paradigm, Natalie is the co-founder and Head of Product at Bottle Bracket, a San Francisco-based wine club. As one of three female cofounders, Natalie has the opportunity to learn about diversity-related challenges in the startup world first hand. Her biggest accomplishment to date is hiring three women developers to lead the technical side of the company!

Natalie serves on the Board of Directors for Friendfactor, a San Francisco-based LGBT nonprofit organization, and has spoken on the topic of diversity and unconscious bias at various conferences (SIOP, Out4Work), Meetups, and alumni events. She is a graduate of Columbia University's Teachers College and Oregon State University where she was a two year co-captain for the Division I NCAA softball program.

Carissa Romero

Partner

As a Partner at Paradigm, Carissa focuses on applying psychological research to solve real-world problems. She has a Ph.D. in Psychology from Stanford University and is an expert in “learning mindsets” - the beliefs that lead people to engage with their work and persist through challenges. Carissa leverages her background in psychology to design and implement data-driven diversity strategies.

While earning her Ph.D. Carissa co-founded PERTS, Stanford’s applied research center on learning mindsets, where she translated research into actionable advice and tools to improve student academic achievement across the country. At Paradigm, she uses her expertise in translating research to practice to inform companies’ diversity and inclusion initiatives.

Carissa is a member of Stanford University’s Mindset Scholars Network and a frequent speaker. She has given hundreds of presentations on mindsets to tens of thousands of people across the country. Her work has been featured in dozens of media outlets, including the New York Times, the Wall Street Journal, and Education Week.

Aleah Warren

Managing Consultant

As Paradigm’s Managing Consultant, Aleah builds and manages client relationships. Before joining Paradigm Aleah was an organizational consultant at UC Berkeley’s Campus Shared Services division, a new division Aleah helped build. In her role as an internal consultant, she advised various project teams on change management and employee engagement strategies. Over the two years that Aleah was with the division, she helped grow the team from 400 to 800 employees.

Aleah brings an organizational psychology lens to change management, and loves helping organizations through growth and transition. She is passionate about helping all employees feel included and engaged at work and is excited to apply her expertise to helping innovative companies become more diverse and inclusive.

Aleah holds a master's degree in Social-Organizational Psychology from Teachers College at Columbia University. She has worked in the non-profit, private, and government sectors, and is a Bay Area native.

Sean Malahy

Data Analyst

As a Data Analyst, Sean uses his statistical background to delve into organizational data and provide a detailed picture of employees’ experiences, as well as companies’ diversity realities, and progress towards diversity goals. Sean uses his research and theoretical training to make recommendations for evidence-based changes to organizational culture, process, and structure.

He gained experimental and analytical expertise from the PhD program at Stanford’s Graduate School of Business, where he designed and executed experimental work in the areas of organizational behavior and social psychology, focusing especially on diversity, ethics, and relationships in work environments.

Before joining Paradigm, Sean was a PhD fellow at Stanford’s Center for Philanthropy and Civil Society. At PACS, he worked with scholars from economics, sociology, political philosophy, and law to develop a better understanding and application of research to meet the needs of civil society. His experience instilled a passion for using multidisciplinary research and data analytics to address issues of justice and fairness in society.
